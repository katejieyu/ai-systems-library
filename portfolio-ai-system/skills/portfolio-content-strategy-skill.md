# Skill — Portfolio Content Strategy

## Purpose

Define a clear, consistent **content strategy** for the portfolio.

This skill determines:
- what to say
- what NOT to say
- what to emphasize
- how to structure information

It ensures all pages reinforce:
- a strong hiring signal
- a consistent positioning
- a clear narrative

---

## When to Use

Use when:

- building or restructuring a portfolio
- improving positioning (Senior → Staff / Principal)
- content feels inconsistent or unclear
- after site scan but before execution
- before rewriting homepage or case studies

Do NOT use:

- when only fixing small UI issues
- before understanding the portfolio intent

---

## Skill Flow

```
scan-site → content-strategy → consistency → execution
```

---

## Instructions

### Step 1 — Define Target Positioning

Determine:

- Target level (Senior / Staff / Principal)
- Core identity (Product / AI / Systems / Founder)
- Key strengths to highlight

---

### Step 2 — Define Core Narrative

Answer:

- Who is this person?
- What do they uniquely do?
- Why are they valuable?

Create a 1–2 sentence positioning statement.

---

### Step 3 — Define Content Pillars

Define 3–5 key themes.

These must:

- appear across pages
- reinforce positioning
- avoid fragmentation

---

### Step 4 — Define Signal Hierarchy

Rank importance:

1. Must be visible immediately (homepage)
2. Must be reinforced across pages
3. Supporting / optional

---

### Step 5 — Define What to REMOVE

Identify:

- low-signal content
- repeated content
- abstract / vague language
- unnecessary frameworks

---

### Step 6 — Define Tone & Style Strategy

Define:

- level of abstraction (prefer clarity over theory)
- sentence style (direct vs conceptual)
- how impact is expressed
- preferred writing patterns

---

### Step 7 — Define Section Strategy

Define:

- homepage structure priorities
- what goes above the fold
- what belongs in case pages
- what should NOT be on homepage

---

### Step 8 — Define CTA Strategy

Define:

- CTA tone (actionable, specific)
- placement strategy
- consistent wording pattern
